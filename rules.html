League Rules

LEAGUE SETTINGS

PRIZE POOL
There are three different prize categories in the league, the regular season leader and the championship runner-up both get their initial entry fee back (currently $50) while the championship winner gets the remaining pot (currently $500). It's possible for the Champion to also earn the regular season prize.

EAST VS. WEST
The two divisions are divided purely based on the regular season standings of the previous fantasy year.

ACQUISTIONS/WAIVERS
Two aquistions are alotted each week, they are refunded as soon as the next match-up begins on Monday. When a player is dropped they are placed on waivers temporarily, adding a player who is on waiver will send you to the bottom of the waiver list, even if no one else is trying to claim the player.

Trades

TRADE VETO
We don't use the ESPN trade veto system, if there is an extremely lopsided or suspicious trade the league will vote on the trade in question. There needs to be at least 6 people who veto the trade in order for the trade to be reversed. Trade vetos are rare instances and not used heavily in the league. It's up to the GMs to do their research prior to trading.

COLLUSION
So far the league has not had any serious collussion incidents but it is something that if discovered will be punished very severely. Any GMs caught colluding or compromising the integrity of the league will face anything from a season ban to permenant

TRADE INJURIES
In the past there have been occurances of injuries happening while a trade is being processed, the precedent set by the league is that as soon as the trade is accepted it can no longer be reverted due to injuries, the trade will go through as normal. The onus is on the GMs to make sure players they are trading for aren't currently injured.

TRADE DEADLINE
The trade deadline is a very important event in fantasy just like it is in the real NHL. Every year the league has a 48 hour period starting exactly one day before, and ending one day after the NHL trade deadline (Ex. NHL Trade deadline is Feb. 27th, BCFHL trade deadline is Feb. 26th-28th). During this time any and all trades made are instantly pushed through by the LM, it's heavily encouraged to do any last minute revisions to your team before playoffs as all trades after the deadline will be veto'd. There is usually a trade event held for everyone to discuss and help push through trades.

Playoffs

PLAYOFF FORMAT
The basic playoff format is heavily determined by your final position in the regular season, no acquisitions are allowed to be made during playoffs (unless injured).All playoff rounds last for one week, the loser(s) (or winner(s) in the case of the Henicup) are eliminated from the playoffs and are finished until the following year. The seed positions are as follows:

Seeds 1-2 will receive a bye week straight into the semi-finals.
Seeds 3-4 will get placed directly into the playoff quarterfinals where they will later play against the winners of the Gaunlet round.
Seeds 5-8 will go into a single-match elimination bracket known as the gauntlet. The winner of the gauntlet round will advance to the quarterfinals of the playoffs, losers are eliminated.
Seeds 9-12 will be in Henicup contention and there will be one round of matches to determine the teams playing in the Henicup Finals.

GAUNTLET
The Gauntlet was introduced in the 2016, prior to the change teams that ended the regular season in the middle of the pack simply had their season ended. As a way to make seeding more competitive while also giving those playoff bubble teams a chance to claw their way up to the cup. The Gauntlet round takes place one week before regular season playoffs.

HENICUP
The Henicup has long served as the anti-championship of the league, the worst teams in the league battle it out to prove that they don't suck as much as at least one other person in the league. Four teams initially enter the Henicup bracket based on seeding, one semi-final round is played between them. The winners are effectively eliminated and safe from winning the Henicup while the losers advance to the final match where the loser goes on to win the Henicup. The Henicup bracket mirrors the regular championship bracket and both finals occur simultaneously.

PLAYOFF INJURIES
During the playoffs injures of any form can severely hamper a team's chance of success, sometimes victory means throwing your star player to the wayside for some who can get points immediately. As such any player who is injured, whether it be OUT, IR, or even DTD can be swapped out for a new player at any time during the playoffs. Acquisitions are not allowed under any other circumstances.
